4. Practice-specific safety

Cold exposure

  • Use conservative temperatures and durations. The app's durations are ceilings or habit prompts, not medical doses or targets that must be completed.
  • Breathe normally. Never combine hyperventilation or breath-holding with water, a shower, bathing, swimming, driving, or any situation in which loss of consciousness could cause harm.
  • Do not perform open-water or ice immersion alone. The app's cold-shower content does not certify conditions as safe for immersion.
  • Stop immediately for chest pain, breathing difficulty, confusion, severe shivering, loss of coordination, panic, dizziness, numbness, or unusual distress. Warm up gradually and seek help when needed.

Sauna and heat

  • Do not use sauna while impaired by alcohol, recreational drugs, severe dehydration, fever, acute illness, or medication that makes heat exposure unsafe.
  • Leave immediately for dizziness, nausea, chest pain, confusion, faintness, breathing difficulty, or distress. Rest and cool gradually.
  • Follow the facility's rules and never treat an app timer as permission to remain in unsafe heat.

Breathing exercises

  • Practice seated or lying down in a safe place. Never practice while driving, operating machinery, in or near water, or where fainting could cause injury.
  • Stop if you feel faint, numb, confused, panicked, or unwell. Norwaymaxxing does not prescribe clinical breathwork.

Nutrition and nature

  • Meal ideas are general examples, not individual dietary prescriptions. Check allergens and adapt portions and ingredients to your needs.
  • Use clinical guidance for allergies, eating disorders, metabolic conditions, pregnancy, medication interactions, and medically prescribed diets.
  • Assess weather, terrain, water, fire, wildlife, daylight, local rules, and your own ability before outdoor activity. Leave no trace and do not enter unsafe or restricted areas.
